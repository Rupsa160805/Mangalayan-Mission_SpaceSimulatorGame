Mangalyaan Mission

India’s First Interplanetary Mission to Mars

Mission Overview

Mangalyaan, officially the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), was launched on 5 November 2013 by ISRO. India became the first Asian country to reach Mars orbit in its first attempt and the fourth space agency in the world to do so.

Mission Objectives

Primary Technological Goal

Design, realise, and launch a Mars Orbiter spacecraft capable of autonomous operation during the journey to Mars, achieving Mars orbit insertion, and performing operations in the Mars orbit phase.

Scientific Goals

  • Study Martian surface features
  • Analyze morphology
  • Study mineralogy
  • Analyze Martian atmosphere
  • Carry five scientific payloads for detailed observations

Key Facts

Launch Date5 November 2013
Mars Orbit Insertion24 September 2014

Launch Mass1337 kg
Mission Cost₹450 Crore (~$74 million)
AchievementsFirst Asian country to reach Mars orbit in first attempt; Successfully sent images of Mars

Mission Timeline

  • 5 Nov 2013 – Launch from Sriharikota
  • 24 Sep 2014 – Mars Orbit Insertion
  • Oct 2014 onwards – First images from Mars
  • Ongoing – Data collection & transmission to Earth

Fun Facts

  • Cost-effective mission – Less than a Hollywood movie budget
  • Lightweight spacecraft designed with indigenous technology
  • India’s first interplanetary mission with successful Mars orbit insertion on first attempt
